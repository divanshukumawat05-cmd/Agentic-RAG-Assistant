# Internship Program HR Policy

## Company Overview

Northstar Digital Solutions Pvt. Ltd. is a global technology services company specializing in enterprise analytics, cloud platforms, and AI-enabled business solutions. The internship program is designed to provide students and recent graduates with practical exposure to real-world business processes, engineering standards, and cross-functional collaboration. Interns are expected to contribute meaningfully, learn continuously, and uphold the professional values of the organization.

The company welcomes interns from diverse academic backgrounds, including computer science, data science, business analytics, electronics, and related disciplines. All interns are treated as temporary contributors and are expected to comply with company policies, confidentiality obligations, and workplace ethics.

## Working Hours

The standard working hours for interns are from 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday through Friday. A lunch break of 45 minutes is provided between 1:00 PM and 2:00 PM. Interns are expected to be available during core business hours from 10:30 AM to 5:30 PM for meetings, collaboration, and project support.

Interns are expected to maintain punctuality and remain reachable on company communication platforms during working hours. Any deviation from the schedule must be approved in advance by the reporting mentor or HR coordinator.

## Attendance Policy

Attendance is a critical requirement for all interns. Interns are expected to maintain at least 95% attendance during the internship period. Late arrival, early departure, or absenteeism without prior approval may affect the intern's evaluation and eligibility for a completion certificate.

Interns must mark attendance through the designated attendance system or submit a manual attendance record in case of access issues. In the event of illness or personal emergency, the intern must notify the mentor and HR team as soon as possible.

## Leave Policy

Interns are entitled to a maximum of 10 leave days during a six-month internship period. Leave requests should be submitted at least 24 hours in advance, except in emergencies. Sick leave may be granted upon submission of a valid medical note.

Leave balances are not transferable to future internship cycles or converted into cash. Excessive leave without proper approval may lead to extension of the internship period or termination of the engagement.

## Work From Home Policy

Work from home (WFH) may be granted for up to two days per week after the first 30 days of the internship, subject to project requirements and mentor approval. WFH requests must be submitted before the start of the week and should not disrupt ongoing team deliverables or meetings.

WFH is not considered a default arrangement. Interns are expected to maintain the same level of productivity, communication, and professionalism while working remotely. The company may revoke WFH privileges in cases of poor performance, missed deadlines, or inconsistent availability.

## Stipend Policy

Interns are eligible to receive a monthly stipend based on their department, role, and performance. The stipend is paid by the 7th business day of each month subject to timely attendance, completion of assigned tasks, and compliance with company policy.

The stipend may be revised based on performance review outcomes, project contribution, or business requirements. Interns are responsible for ensuring that their bank account and KYC details are updated in the HR system before the first stipend cycle.

## Code of Conduct

All interns are expected to follow professional workplace conduct at all times. This includes being respectful to colleagues, supervisors, and clients; maintaining a positive attitude; using company assets responsibly; and participating in team activities constructively.

Interns must not engage in harassment, discrimination, gossip, unauthorized sharing of internal information, or conduct that could damage the reputation of the organization. Any concerns related to workplace conduct should be escalated promptly to the mentor or HR representative.

## Confidentiality Policy

Interns are required to protect all non-public information belonging to the company, its clients, and its partners. Confidential information includes internal documents, business strategies, client data, source code, product plans, financial information, and access credentials.

Interns must not share confidential information with third parties or use it for personal benefit. Upon completion of the internship, interns must return all company property and delete any local copies of confidential materials unless explicitly authorized in writing.

## Performance Evaluation

Performance will be reviewed periodically by the assigned mentor and the relevant functional lead. Evaluation criteria include technical contribution, communication, reliability, problem-solving ability, time management, and collaboration.

Interns will receive a mid-point review and a final review. The final performance rating will influence whether the intern receives a certificate of completion, a recommendation letter, or a future opportunity for full-time employment.

## Mentor Communication

Each intern will be assigned a mentor who will provide task guidance, review progress, and support professional development. Interns are expected to attend weekly one-on-one meetings, maintain regular status updates, and raise blockers promptly.

Communication should be professional, concise, and solution-oriented. Interns must respond to mentor messages within one business day and update their work progress in the prescribed project tracker.

## Holidays

Interns are entitled to observe public holidays and company-declared holidays as per the organization's holiday calendar. Holiday schedules will be communicated in advance by the HR team. Interns are expected to comply with the approved calendar and plan personal travel accordingly.

If a holiday falls on a working day for a project with critical deliverables, the mentor may require attendance after prior approval from the department lead.

## Internship Completion Rules

To successfully complete the internship, interns must fulfill attendance requirements, complete assigned work items, submit all required deliverables, and participate in the final review process. Failure to meet these requirements may result in an incomplete internship record or non-issuance of a completion certificate.

Upon completion, interns must return any company-issued devices, complete an exit feedback form, and confirm that all confidential information has been handled appropriately. The company reserves the right to extend, terminate, or modify an internship based on performance and policy compliance.
